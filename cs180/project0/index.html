Part 1: Selfie: The Wrong Way vs. The Right Way

In this part, I took a picture of myself from close up with a short focal length. Then, I took a few steps back, zoomed in (increase focal length) and take another picture with the same size with the first picture. Although the two pictures are in the same size, the close-up one with wide-angle lens makes my face distorted with my nose appeared larger, while the zoomed-in one with far distance results a more natural and flat view.

Part 2: Architectural Perspective Compression

I repeat the same process from Part 1 to take pictures of an architecture. In the results, the picture taken zoomed-in from far distance has the lines of house straight up and flat, while the picture taken close-up has lines tiled.

Part 3: The Dolly Zoom

To view the difference of pictures taken with different focal lengths, I create a scene with the dolly zoom effect, by taking multiple frames of the sunflower, zooming in and walking away at the same time. With the sunflower stays at the same size, the background size gets smaller.
